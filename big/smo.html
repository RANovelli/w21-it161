Social Media Optimization (SMO)

Social Media Optimization (SMO) is a great, low-cost way to increase your business’s reach through social media platforms. According to Mike Shaw at TowerMarketing.net, “45% of the world’s population uses social media and spends more than 140 minutes a day surfing. First-world and third-world countries alike lean on the power of social media. If you want your business to be successful, optimizing your website and social channels isn’t a choice. It’s a necessity” (Shaw). This shows that optimizing social media content and strategy can greatly increase your overall business success in the global economy.

SMO is shown to generate more business because people are using social media regularly for work, and they feel more comfortable endorsing a product trusted by a peer. Lydia Roth, on the Alexa blog, says, “Social media is successful because it gives people a social outlet on the internet. But social media isn’t just for fun – 67% of people use social media for work, according to a survey by Bambu by Sprout Social. Additionally, people are more likely to connect with people they already know than with brands. In fact, people are 16 times more likely to read a post about a brand from a friend than from the brand itself” (Roth).

There are many ways to use SMO, and coming up with a good strategy is key to wide visibility. Macy Storm at WebFX.com states, “Your strategy should focus on growing your business’s presence and achieving measurable results. It’s vital that you focus on goals you can monitor and measure to ensure you’re driving success with your campaign” (Storm). There are a variety of analytic tools on the market, some of them at no cost, to assist in assessing what works well, and what does not. Relevant topics, keywords, and hashtags all play important parts in driving more people to your page.
